Our client's hiring manager was spending 12 hours a week sorting applications.

Not interviewing. Not making decisions. Just… sorting.

She had an intake form that dumped to her inbox. 80+ applications per role, most of them unqualified. She'd read each one, move it to a folder, repeat. By the time she got to the good candidates, she was already exhausted.

We built her a hiring system:

→ Applicant submits intake form
→ AI scores each response against a rubric (skills, experience, culture fit)
→ Top 20% auto-tagged and surfaced in a Notion tracker
→ Calendly link auto-sent to qualified candidates
→ Rejection email auto-sent to everyone else

Now she spends 45 minutes a week on hiring admin — reviewing the shortlist, confirming interviews, making decisions.

The 11+ hours she recovered went back to billable work and client delivery.

This isn't about cutting corners. It's about keeping smart people focused on the work only they can do.
