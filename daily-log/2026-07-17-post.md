## Draft

We built a reporting system for a client. Six months later, they'd gotten back 104 hours.

Here's what that looked like:

**Before:**
Every week: open spreadsheet. Pull data manually from 3 tools. Format it. Send it. Hope it was right.
4 hours. Every week. Same client. Same report.

When it was late — and it was late — the client noticed. Late reports create friction. Friction creates churn risk.

**What we built:**
Automated pull from their existing tools → clean formatted report → scheduled Slack delivery.

Zero manual steps. The report lands before the client even thinks to ask.

**What changed:**
4 hours/week × 26 weeks = 104 hours returned to the team.

Reports arrive on time, every time. That client is still with them.

The return on automation isn't always cost savings.
Sometimes it's just getting your team's attention back — and keeping a client you'd have otherwise lost.
